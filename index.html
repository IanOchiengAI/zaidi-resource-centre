ABOUT US

Our History

Founded in 2013 as Muscular Dystrophy Society Kenya. Formally registered in 2025.

Grounded in Disability Justice Principles: Interdependence, Collective Liberation, and Wholeness.

Co-convener of Rare Disease Day celebrations in Kenya from 2014 to 2017 alongside Stepping Stones Kenya and Gertrude’s Children’s Hospital Foundation.

The Team

Faith Njahĩra Wangarĩ

Co-Founder & Executive Director

Feminist scholar, author, researcher and disability inclusion expert with muscular dystrophy. Member of the Global Limb Girdle Muscular Dystrophy Community Advisory Board. Global consultant on disability, gender and organising. Masters in Disability Studies from Syracuse University-USA.

Margaret Wangarĩ Gachĩgĩ

Co-Founder & Operations Director

Educator & Administrator. 36 years of public service, including 27 years as secondary school Principal. Court Accredited Mediator (2023) and certified by CREA Disability, Sexuality and Rights Online Institute-East Africa (2025). Bachelor’s degree in Education from Kenyatta University.

OUR PILLARS

Education & Training

Training teachers/educators, healthcare professionals of all cadres, religious/spiritual practitioners and other relevant stakeholders on muscular dystrophy and other disabilities.

Research

Bridging knowledge gaps on muscular dystrophy and disability using feminist and decolonial approaches. Prioritising lived experiences and centering community voices.

Wholeness

Mind, body, and spirit wellness. Focus on play, grief and mourning, music, and dance. Creating spaces for joy, healing, and authentic expression.

Together, these pillars create a foundation for lasting change—one that honors the full humanity of every person.
